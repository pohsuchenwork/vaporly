## What Vaporly can do on your Mac

So there are no surprises, here is an honest description of the capabilities
Vaporly uses to do its job:

- It records your microphone while you are dictating.
- It monitors keyboard input so it can detect the push-to-talk hotkey you
  registered. It watches for that one hotkey only. It is not a keylogger and
  does not record what you type.
- It can paste text into whatever application is focused, by sending synthetic
  keystrokes through the macOS Accessibility system.

These abilities exist to make dictation work. Vaporly uses them locally and for
that purpose only. For details on how they are handled, see
[SECURITY.md](../.github/SECURITY.md) and [PRIVACY.md](PRIVACY.md).
